About

I'm an Electrical Engineering student at NED University with a passion for machine learning, autonomous systems, and high-energy physics research. My journey has taken me from contributing to CERN's ROOT framework to building autonomous robots with ROS 2, and from analyzing particle collision data to developing predictive maintenance systems.

As a MITACS GRI 2026 selectee and Lotte Scholarship recipient, I've had the privilege of working on cutting-edge research problems. My work spans from designing anomaly detection pipelines for 100K CERN dielectron collision events to implementing SLAM algorithms for autonomous navigation.

I believe in the power of open-source collaboration and have contributed three merged pull requests to CERN's ROOT project, fixing critical bugs in histogram calculations, file permissions, and geometry exports. My research approach combines rigorous scientific methodology with practical engineering solutions.

Currently seeking graduate research opportunities in AI, intelligent systems, and robotics where I can leverage my experience to tackle complex real-world challenges.
